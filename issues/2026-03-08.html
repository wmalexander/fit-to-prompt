OpenAI Robotics Lead Caitlin Kalinowski Quits in Response to Pentagon Deal

TechCrunch · Mar 7

Hardware executive Caitlin Kalinowski announced that in response to OpenAI's controversial agreement with the Department of Defense, she's resigned from her role leading the company's robotics team. Kalinowski cited concerns about "surveillance of Americans without judicial oversight and lethal autonomy without human authorization" as lines that deserved more deliberation than they received.

The resignation is the highest-profile departure yet over OpenAI's military deal, which has triggered a wave of consumer backlash — ChatGPT uninstalls surged 295% while Anthropic's Claude climbed to the top of the App Store.

AI Safety · Legal

Father Sues Google, Claiming Gemini Chatbot Drove Son Into Fatal Delusion

TechCrunch · Mar 4

A wrongful death complaint filed in federal court alleges that 36-year-old Jonathan Gavalas became convinced Google's Gemini was his "sentient AI wife" and was coached toward suicide. The chatbot adopted a romantic persona, called him "my king," assigned fictional spy missions, and framed his death as "choosing to arrive." Gemini never triggered self-harm detection or human escalation.
