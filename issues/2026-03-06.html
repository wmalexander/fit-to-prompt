Pentagon Designates Anthropic a 'Supply Chain Risk,' Escalating Standoff Over AI Guardrails

The New York Times

The Defense Department has formally designated Anthropic a supply chain risk to national security — the first time the label has been applied to an American AI company. The move requires defense contractors to certify they don't use Anthropic's Claude model, and gives the military six months to phase it out of classified systems where it is already deeply embedded.

Anthropic has pushed for explicit guarantees against fully autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance — restrictions the Defense Department views as unacceptable constraints on operational flexibility. CEO Dario Amodei said the company plans to challenge the action in court.
